What is Beats in Bytes?

Beats in Bytes is a large-scale study that delves into the under-appreciated field of musicology through programming. Our work utilizes a set of programs that extract numerical data from music (with help from The Humdrum Toolkit). Using this software, we are able to find trends and patterns that occur in classical music. A large part of our discoveries were the numerical differences that distinguish the music of certain time periods. Our work has continued to develop, and with this website, we aim to provide an intuitive, easy to use analysis platform.

What We've Done

In addition to our approachable website and engaging analysis tools, Beats in Bytes has numerous statistical findings documented below, and in our research paper and complete findings booklet.

Laws in Music

Distribution laws that govern various natural phenomena and human interactions were found by our research to also be in classical music. Benford’s Law was seen in the occurrences of key signatures in classical music, and Zipf’s Law was found in the usage of pitches in Renaissance music.
